The quip water flosser is a complex electromechanical system integrating a DC Motor, Lithium Ion Battery, Pump System with detailed stamped metal finishes. I was responsible for the design and refinement of the product from Alpha Prototype to Mass Production.

Key Activities:

  • Waterproofing and gasket refinement to achieve IPX7
  • Charging contact corrosion resistance and plating specifications
  • Metal stamping design and assembly
  • Step / gap minimization at key part junctions
  • Assembly process flow refinement for proper part alignment and centering

Custom insert molded stainless steel charging contacts. Key challenges involved resisting corrosion, preventing flash, plating strength after cycling with magnetic charger. Shown mating with custom quip USB magnetic charger.

Stamped aluminum outer shell and die cut buttons. Key challenges involved reliably centering the buttons with a uniform step, preventing step and gap with plastic after assembly.

Magnetic floss tip to allow for 360 degrees of rotation. Key design challenges included plating magnet to resist impact and withstand salt spray corrosion testing.

Internal plastic housing with button over mold and holes for LED light pipes. Key challenges involved tuning button press force, water ingress at light pipe interface and mating with aluminum shell.
